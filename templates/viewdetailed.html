Melanoma, the most serious type of skin cancer, develops in the cells (melanocytes) that produce melanin — the pigment that gives your skin its color. Melanoma can also form in your eyes and, rarely, inside your body, such as in your nose or throat. The exact cause of all melanomas isn't clear, but exposure to ultraviolet (UV) radiation from sunlight or tanning lamps and beds increases your risk of developing melanoma.

Recommended medicine:

  • Protect the affected area from further sun exposure.
  • Apply pressure to any bleeding: If the melanoma is bleeding, apply firm, direct pressure to the affected area with a clean cloth or bandage until the bleeding stops.
  • Keep the affected area clean and dry: Clean the area with mild soap and water, and pat dry with a clean towel.

  • Do not attempt to remove the melanoma: Trying to remove the melanoma yourself can cause it to bleed or become infected, which can further complicate treatment.
